## SOURCE PIECE

### Five workflows a small team can hand to AI (before hiring anyone new)

Most small teams don't have a headcount problem. They have a repetition problem — the same fifteen tasks eating the same hours every week. These five are the ones worth automating first, because they're frequent, low-risk, and easy to check.

**1. Meeting notes into action items.** Record the call, let AI produce the summary, decisions, and owner-tagged action list, then spend two minutes correcting it instead of twenty writing it. The meeting doesn't get shorter; the follow-up does.

**2. First drafts of support replies.** AI drafts the response from your past answers and docs; a human reads, fixes, and sends. Customers still get a person — the person just starts at 80% instead of zero.

**3. Content repurposing.** One good piece of source material — a project write-up, a talk, a blog post — can become a LinkedIn post, a thread, a carousel, and a newsletter blurb. AI does the reformatting; a human keeps the facts and the voice honest. (This article was built to be run through exactly that workflow.)

**4. Lead research.** Turning "companies that might need us" into a structured list — name, site, what they do, why they fit — is hours of tab-switching that AI compresses into minutes. Humans then qualify the list instead of building it.

**5. Reporting.** Numbers from analytics into a readable weekly summary: what moved, what didn't, what to try next. AI writes the narrative; a human checks the numbers and owns the conclusions.

The pattern across all five: **AI drafts, humans decide.** Automation buys back the hours; judgment stays where it belongs. Teams that get this split right ship faster without trusting a machine with anything that matters unchecked.
