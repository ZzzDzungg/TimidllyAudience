## 1. Where this fits — the funnel

The cohort's North Star is **Passionfroot bookings** (brands booking placements through the Timidlly storefront). Every track owns one stage of the same funnel:

**Reach** (Audience — Dũng) → **Visits** (Inbound — nikita; Conversion — Shriyan) → **Inquiries** (Conversion — Shriyan + Hyeonseok) → **Bookings** (Pipeline — Devrath + Raghav)

The Audience track owns the top of that funnel. The content engine has two jobs:

1. **Grow reach and posting consistency** so Timidlly's channels look active, credible, and valuable — this is what brands are ultimately buying, and it's the raw material for Hyeonseok's media kit and Devrath's outreach.
2. **Route attention downstream** — content should regularly point people toward the funnel pages and storefront that the Conversion and Inbound tracks are building.

## 2. Audience vs. customer

These are two different groups and the spec treats them separately:

- **The audience** = the people who follow and read Timidlly's content (working assumption: a tech / AI / startup-interested audience, based on the company's positioning — *to confirm with Somitra*).
- **The customer** = brands that book sponsorships/placements via Passionfroot. They don't need to love the content; they need evidence the audience is real, growing, and engaged.

Content decisions serve the audience; measurement and reporting serve the customer story.

## 3. What the "content engine" is

A repeatable system — not a pile of one-off posts: **source material in → multi-platform content batch out → scheduled → measured → repeated weekly.**

## 5. Workflow

1. **Ideation** — topics from audience interest, top-performing past posts, and what the team is shipping
2. **Creation** — draft/produce the asset; AI-assisted drafting and repurposing (one source piece → several platform-native posts) is the core efficiency play, with human review always in the loop
3. **Review** — internal check before anything publishes (confirm with Somitra who approves)
4. **Publish** — scheduled to a set weekly cadence
5. **Distribute** — cross-post, reshare, and link toward the funnel/storefront
6. **Measure** — log reach and engagement in the tracker; feed results back into ideation

Tooling (scheduler, AI-assist stack, tracking method) is deliberately not locked in this spec — evaluating and choosing tools is the Week 3 skeleton work.

## 6. Cadence

Baseline cadence is being recorded this week (see tracker). A target cadence will be proposed in Week 4 once the Week 3 batch shows real per-piece effort. Placeholder for discussion: 3–5 pieces/week on primary channels, sustained, rather than bursts.

## 7. KPIs owned by this track

- **Followers / subscribers per channel** (proxy for reach; snapshot monthly in the Update Log)
- **Average engagement per post and engagement rate**
- **Posts per week** (consistency is itself a KPI)
- **Reach delta vs. Week 2 baseline** — the headline number reported in Weeks 5, 6, and 8

North Star context: these are leading indicators. They matter insofar as they grow storefront visits, inquiries, and ultimately Passionfroot bookings.
